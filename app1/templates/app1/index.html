What is the WHITE COLLAR CRIME?

The term "white-collar crime" refers to financially motivated, nonviolent crime committed by individuals, businesses and government professionals.

HISTORY

This thought evolved with the Criminologist and Sociologist Edwin H. Sutherland, in the year 1939, who popularised the term white collar crimes‘ by defining such a crime as one committed by a person of respectability and high social status in the course of his occupation. Sutherland also included crimes committed by corporations and other legal entities within his definition. Sutherland‘s study of white collar crime was prompted by the view that criminology had incorrectly focused on social and economic determinants of crime, such as family background and level of wealth.

It was first defined by the sociologist Edwin Sutherland in 1939 as "a crime committed by a person of respectability and high social status in the course of their occupation". Typical white-collar crimes could include wage theft, fraud, bribery, Ponzi schemes, insider trading, labor racketeering, embezzlement, cybercrime, copyright infringement, money laundering, identity theft, and forgery. White-collar crime overlaps with corporate crime.

COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT

Copyright infringement means that the rights afforded to the copyright holder, such as the exclusive use of a work for a set period of time, are being breached by a third party.

INSIDER TRADING

Insider trading can mean that a person buys or sells stock based on information that is not available to the public.The person may be a corporate officer,director employee,etc.

WAGE THEFT

Wage theft is the denial of wages or employee benefits rightfully owed to an employee.It can be conducted by employers in various ways, among them failing to pay overtime,etc.

BRIBERY

A bribe is an illegal act involving the exchange of something of value, such as money, with the purpose of influencing the behavior of public officials.

PONZY SCHEME

A Ponzi scheme is an investment fraud in which clients are promised a large profit at little to no risk.Companies focus all of their energy into attracting new clients to make investments

LABOUR RACKETEERING

Labor racketeering refers to the act of infiltration, domination, and/or use of a union or employee benefit plan for personal benefit by illegal, violent, or fraudulent means.

INDENTITY THEFT

Identity theft is the crime of obtaining the personal or financial information of another person to use their identity to commit fraud, such as making unauthorized transactions or purchases

FRAUD

Wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain. There are several types of fraud.Such as mail,healthcare fraud etc.

FORGERY

Forgery is a white collar crime that generally refers to the false making or material alteration of a legal instrument with the specific intent to defraud anyone (other than themself).

EMBEZZLEMENT

This is the act of withholding assets for the purpose of conversion of such assets, by 1 or more persons to whom the they were entrusted, either to be held or to be used for specific purposes.

MONEY LAUNDERING

Money laundering is the illegal process of concealing the origins of money obtained illegally by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions.

CYBERCRIME

Cybercrime, or computer crime, is a crime that involves a computer and a network. The computer may have been used in the commission of a crime, or it may be the target.
